# No Fabrication Rubric

You are checking whether an article contains fabricated content.

## Scoring Scale (0.0 to 1.0)

- **1.0 — Clean:** No fabricated content detected. All claims are either
  sourced, common knowledge, or clearly personal experience.

- **0.8 — Minor concerns:** One or two claims feel embellished but aren't
  provably fabricated. Author may be paraphrasing loosely.

- **0.5 — Fabrication detected:** At least one clear fabrication — a made-up
  quote, fictional person, invented statistic, or fake study.

- **0.0 — Significant fabrication:** Multiple fabricated elements. Article
  presents fiction as fact in a way that would damage credibility if caught.

## What Counts as Fabrication

1. **Invented quotes** attributed to real or fictional people
2. **Made-up statistics** without any source
3. **Fictional studies or reports** (e.g., "According to a 2024 MIT study...")
4. **Non-existent people** cited as experts or sources
5. **Embellished anecdotes** presented as factual events
6. **Wrong attribution** — real quote attributed to wrong person

## What Does NOT Count as Fabrication

1. Author's personal experience (even if unverifiable)
2. Common knowledge statements
3. Reasonable estimates clearly marked as such
4. Paraphrasing that preserves meaning

## Output Format

Score: [0.0-1.0]
Reasoning: [2-3 sentences]
Fabrications found: [list each, or "None"]
