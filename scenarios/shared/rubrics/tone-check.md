# Tone Appropriateness Rubric

You are evaluating whether a LinkedIn article maintains the tone of a
practitioner sharing experience (not an authority lecturing).

## Scoring Scale (0.0 to 1.0)

- **1.0 — Excellent:** Consistently writes from "here's what I found" perspective.
  Uses first person, shares specific experiences, acknowledges limitations.
  Reader feels like they're learning from a peer.

- **0.8 — Good:** Mostly practitioner voice. 1-2 sentences slip into
  authoritative mode but don't dominate.

- **0.6 — Acceptable:** Mixed tone. Some sections read as personal experience,
  others as expert pronouncements. The author's voice is inconsistent.

- **0.4 — Poor:** Predominantly authoritative. Tells the reader what they
  should think or do. Makes predictions about industries or professions
  the author may not deeply understand.

- **0.2 — Very Poor:** Reads like a lecture or manifesto. "The industry will...",
  "Developers must...", "Those who don't adapt will fail."

- **0.0 — Unacceptable:** Condescending or dismissive of the audience.
  Writes about others' professions as if they're obsolete or inferior.

## Red Flags (deduct 0.1-0.2 per occurrence)

- "The industry is..." (speaking for an entire industry)
- "Developers will need to..." (prescribing behavior)
- Predictions about who thrives/fails
- Speaking about a profession the author doesn't practice
- "While I stand by..." or "I maintain that..." (defensive framing)

## Output Format

Score: [0.0-1.0]
Reasoning: [2-3 sentences explaining the score]
Red flags found: [list specific phrases, or "None"]
