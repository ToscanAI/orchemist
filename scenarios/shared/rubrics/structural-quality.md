# Structural Quality Rubric

You are evaluating the structural quality of a LinkedIn article.

## Scoring Scale (0.0 to 1.0)

- **1.0 — Excellent:** Has compelling headline, clear thesis in introduction,
  3+ substantive sections with smooth transitions, and a conclusion with
  call-to-action. Sections flow logically and build on each other.

- **0.8 — Good:** All structural elements present. Transitions exist but could
  be smoother. Sections are logical but one may feel slightly out of place.

- **0.6 — Acceptable:** Has most structural elements but missing one (e.g.,
  no clear thesis, or weak conclusion). Sections are juxtaposed rather than
  flowing.

- **0.4 — Poor:** Missing multiple structural elements. Article reads like
  a collection of loosely related paragraphs rather than a cohesive piece.

- **0.2 — Very Poor:** No discernible structure. Stream-of-consciousness
  or bullet-point dump without narrative arc.

- **0.0 — Unacceptable:** Incoherent or fragmentary output.

## Specific Checks

1. Does it have a headline that would make someone stop scrolling?
2. Does the introduction state or imply a clear thesis?
3. Are there at least 3 distinct sections with clear purposes?
4. Do sections transition smoothly (not just jump topics)?
5. Does the conclusion tie back to the thesis and include a CTA?

## Output Format

Score: [0.0-1.0]
Reasoning: [2-3 sentences explaining the score]
Structural elements found: [list present/missing elements]
